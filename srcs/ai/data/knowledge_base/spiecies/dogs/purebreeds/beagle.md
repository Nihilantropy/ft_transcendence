# Beagle

## Overview
Beagles are merry, friendly hounds originally bred for hunting rabbits. Their excellent sense of smell and tracking instinct make them popular hunting companions and detection dogs. They are one of America's most beloved family pets.

## Temperament
- **Friendly**: Good with children and other dogs
- **Curious**: Driven by nose, loves to explore
- **Merry**: Happy-go-lucky disposition
- **Determined**: Can be stubborn during training
- **Vocal**: Prone to barking and howling

## Physical Characteristics
- **Size**: Small to medium (20-30 lbs, 13-15 inches tall)
- **Coat**: Short, dense, weather-resistant
- **Colors**: Tricolor (black, tan, white), lemon, red and white
- **Ears**: Long, floppy
- **Lifespan**: 12-15 years

## Health Considerations
- **Obesity**: Extremely food-motivated, prone to overeating
- **Hip Dysplasia**: Less common than large breeds but possible
- **Ear Infections**: Floppy ears trap moisture
- **Epilepsy**: Genetic predisposition
- **Hypothyroidism**: Can contribute to weight gain
- **Cherry Eye**: Eyelid condition requiring surgery

## Care Requirements
- **Exercise**: 1 hour daily, secure fencing essential (will follow scents)
- **Grooming**: Weekly brushing, regular ear cleaning critical
- **Diet**: Strict portion control, food-puzzle toys recommended
- **Training**: Positive reinforcement, patience needed
- **Containment**: Never off-leash in unfenced areas

## Special Considerations
- Exceptional escape artists - secure yard required
- Will follow nose regardless of training
- Food-driven - use for training motivation
- Can develop separation anxiety
- May bay/howl when bored or lonely

## Nutrition Recommendations
- Measured meals twice daily (never free-feed)
- High-quality protein (minimum 18-22%)
- Avoid table scraps
- Use low-calorie treats for training
- Monitor weight closely - obesity is the #1 health issue

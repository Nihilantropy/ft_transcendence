# Poodle (Standard, Miniature, Toy)

## Overview
Poodles are exceptionally intelligent, athletic, and versatile dogs. Available in three sizes (Standard, Miniature, Toy), they consistently rank as one of the smartest breeds. Originally bred as water retrievers in Germany.

## Temperament
- **Intelligent**: Second most intelligent breed, highly trainable
- **Active**: Athletic and energetic despite elegant appearance
- **Proud**: Carries themselves with dignity
- **Alert**: Excellent watchdogs
- **People-Oriented**: Bonds strongly with family

## Physical Characteristics
- **Sizes**: 
  - Standard: 45-70 lbs, over 15 inches
  - Miniature: 10-15 lbs, 10-15 inches
  - Toy: 4-6 lbs, under 10 inches
- **Coat**: Curly, dense, hypoallergenic (low-shedding)
- **Colors**: White, black, apricot, cream, silver, brown, parti-color
- **Lifespan**: 12-15 years

## Health Considerations
- **Hip Dysplasia**: Especially in Standard Poodles
- **Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA)**: Genetic eye disease leading to blindness
- **Epilepsy**: More common in Poodles than many breeds
- **Addison's Disease**: Adrenal gland disorder
- **Bloat (GDV)**: Risk in Standard Poodles
- **Luxating Patella**: Kneecap dislocation, more common in Miniature/Toy

## Care Requirements
- **Exercise**: 1 hour daily for Standards, 30-45 minutes for smaller varieties
- **Grooming**: Professional grooming every 6-8 weeks, daily brushing essential
- **Mental Stimulation**: Needs puzzle toys, training, activities
- **Socialization**: Early and ongoing to prevent anxiety
- **Dental Care**: Critical for Toy/Miniature varieties

## Grooming Requirements
- Coat grows continuously and doesn't shed
- Requires professional grooming to prevent matting
- Popular clips: Puppy, Continental, English Saddle, Sporting
- Daily brushing prevents tangles
- Regular ear cleaning (prone to ear infections)
- Tear stain management for lighter colors

## Special Considerations
- Excellent for allergy sufferers (minimal shedding)
- Thrives in dog sports: agility, obedience, dock diving
- Sensitive to harsh training methods
- Can develop separation anxiety if left alone frequently
- Water lovers - natural swimmers

## Nutrition Recommendations
- High-quality food appropriate to size variety
- Standard Poodles: Large breed formula to slow growth
- Toy/Miniature: Small breed formula with smaller kibble
- Avoid foods with artificial colors (can stain coat)
- Omega-3 supplements for coat health

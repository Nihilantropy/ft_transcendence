# German Shepherd

## Overview
German Shepherds are confident, courageous, and intelligent working dogs. Originally bred for herding, they excel in police, military, and service work. They are the second most popular breed in the United States.

## Temperament
- **Loyal**: Deeply devoted to family, protective instincts
- **Intelligent**: Exceptionally smart, quick learners
- **Confident**: Self-assured but not hostile
- **Courageous**: Brave and reliable guardians
- **Alert**: Excellent watchdogs with strong territorial instinct

## Physical Characteristics
- **Size**: Large (50-90 lbs, 22-26 inches tall)
- **Coat**: Double coat, medium length, sheds year-round
- **Colors**: Most commonly tan/black or red/black saddle pattern
- **Lifespan**: 9-13 years

## Health Considerations
- **Hip Dysplasia**: Very common, breeding stock should be tested
- **Elbow Dysplasia**: Monitor for lameness
- **Degenerative Myelopathy**: Progressive spinal cord disease
- **Bloat (GDV)**: Life-threatening stomach condition, requires immediate vet care
- **Exocrine Pancreatic Insufficiency**: Digestive disorder
- **Allergies**: Skin allergies relatively common

## Care Requirements
- **Exercise**: 1-2 hours daily, needs mental stimulation
- **Grooming**: Brush 2-3 times weekly, daily during shedding seasons
- **Diet**: High-quality food, avoid overfeeding, consider large-breed formula
- **Training**: Early socialization critical, thrives with structured training
- **Mental Stimulation**: Needs jobs/tasks to prevent boredom

## Nutrition Recommendations
- Feed measured meals twice daily to prevent bloat
- Avoid exercise immediately before/after meals
- Consider glucosamine supplements for joint health
- Protein-rich diet (22-25% for adults)

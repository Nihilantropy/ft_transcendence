# French Bulldog

## Overview
French Bulldogs are playful, adaptable, and smart companion dogs. Their distinctive bat ears and flat faces make them instantly recognizable. They rank among the top 5 most popular breeds worldwide.

## Temperament
- **Affectionate**: Loves human companionship, excellent lap dogs
- **Playful**: Energetic in short bursts, loves toys
- **Adaptable**: Thrives in apartments and small spaces
- **Sociable**: Gets along with people and other pets
- **Alert**: Good watchdogs despite small size

## Physical Characteristics
- **Size**: Small to medium (16-28 lbs, 11-13 inches tall)
- **Coat**: Short, smooth, minimal shedding
- **Colors**: Brindle, fawn, white, combinations
- **Build**: Compact, muscular, heavy-boned
- **Lifespan**: 10-12 years

## Health Considerations
- **Brachycephalic Syndrome**: Breathing difficulties due to flat face
- **Heat Sensitivity**: Cannot regulate temperature well, prone to overheating
- **Spinal Disorders**: Intervertebral disc disease (IVDD)
- **Eye Problems**: Cherry eye, corneal ulcers
- **Skin Fold Dermatitis**: Facial wrinkles need regular cleaning
- **Allergies**: Food and environmental allergies common

## Care Requirements
- **Exercise**: 15-30 minutes daily, avoid strenuous activity
- **Temperature Control**: Keep cool, avoid hot weather, never leave in car
- **Grooming**: Weekly brushing, clean facial folds daily
- **Diet**: Portion control essential, prone to obesity
- **Breathing Monitoring**: Watch for labored breathing, gagging

## Special Considerations
- Cannot swim well due to body structure
- Anesthesia requires special protocols due to airway anatomy
- May require cesarean sections for breeding
- Avoid stair climbing when possible to protect spine
- Use harness instead of collar to prevent breathing issues

## Nutrition Recommendations
- Small, frequent meals to prevent bloat
- High-quality protein (22-26%)
- Limited ingredient diets if allergies present
- Avoid overfeeding - obesity worsens breathing problems

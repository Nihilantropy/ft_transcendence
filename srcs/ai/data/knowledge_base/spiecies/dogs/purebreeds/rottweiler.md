# Rottweiler

## Overview
Rottweilers are loyal, loving, and confident guardians. Originally bred to drive cattle and pull carts for butchers in Germany, they now excel as family protectors, police dogs, and therapy animals.

## Temperament
- **Loyal**: Deeply devoted to family
- **Confident**: Self-assured, not easily startled
- **Protective**: Natural guardian instinct
- **Calm**: Composed and observant
- **Affectionate**: Gentle with family, especially children when properly socialized

## Physical Characteristics
- **Size**: Large (80-135 lbs, 22-27 inches tall)
- **Build**: Robust, powerful, muscular
- **Coat**: Short, dense, black with rust markings
- **Lifespan**: 8-10 years

## Health Considerations
- **Hip Dysplasia**: Very common, breeding stock must be tested
- **Elbow Dysplasia**: Also prevalent
- **Osteosarcoma**: Bone cancer risk higher than average
- **Bloat (GDV)**: Life-threatening, requires immediate care
- **Heart Issues**: Subaortic stenosis, dilated cardiomyopathy
- **Eye Problems**: Progressive retinal atrophy, cataracts
- **Cruciate Ligament Tears**: ACL injuries common

## Care Requirements
- **Exercise**: 1-2 hours daily, loves pulling activities
- **Training**: Early socialization CRITICAL, professional training recommended
- **Grooming**: Weekly brushing, more during shedding seasons
- **Mental Stimulation**: Needs jobs/tasks, excels at obedience work
- **Containment**: Secure fencing, supervision around strangers

## Training Considerations
- Early socialization (8-16 weeks) is non-negotiable
- Positive reinforcement works best
- Consistent leadership required
- Natural protectiveness needs channeling
- Excellent candidates for advanced obedience, protection sports
- Not recommended for first-time dog owners

## Special Considerations
- Requires experienced, confident handler
- Strong prey drive - careful introductions to other pets
- Can be same-sex aggressive
- Needs clear boundaries and rules
- Banned or restricted in some areas
- Insurance considerations for homeowners

## Nutrition Recommendations
- Large breed puppy formula to slow growth (prevents joint issues)
- Feed twice daily to reduce bloat risk
- High-quality protein (minimum 22-26%)
- Glucosamine and chondroitin for joint health
- Avoid exercise immediately before/after meals
- Monitor weight - obesity stresses joints

## Warning Signs
- Limping or lameness (joint problems)
- Reluctance to rise or climb stairs
- Swollen abdomen, unproductive vomiting (bloat emergency)
- Lethargy or weakness (heart issues)

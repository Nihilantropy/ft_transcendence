# British Shorthair

## Overview
The British Shorthair is one of the oldest and most pedigreed cat breeds in the world, descended from cats brought to Britain by the Romans. Famous for their plush teddy-bear appearance, round faces, and calm demeanor, they are consistently one of the most popular breeds in Europe. The British Shorthair was the inspiration for the Cheshire Cat in Alice in Wonderland.

## Temperament
- **Calm**: One of the most laid-back cat breeds; not overly demanding or hyperactive
- **Adaptable**: Adjusts well to apartment life, families with children, and multi-pet households
- **Independent**: Affectionate but not clingy; does not need constant attention
- **Gentle**: Patient and tolerant; rarely scratches or bites even when handled by children
- **Moderate Playfulness**: Enjoys interactive play but not as active as Oriental breeds; mellows with age
- **Reserved with Strangers**: Polite but takes time to warm up to new people

## Physical Characteristics
- **Size**: Medium to large (7–17 lbs; males significantly larger than females)
- **Build**: Compact, cobby, muscular body with a broad chest and short, thick neck
- **Coat**: Short, dense, plush double coat that stands away from the body — the hallmark "teddy bear" texture
- **Face**: Broad, round head with full cheeks, round eyes, and a short, broad nose
- **Colors**: Historically "British Blue" (solid grey-blue) is most iconic; modern breeding includes black, white, cream, tabby, bicolor, colorpoint, and many more
- **Eyes**: Large, round; typically copper or gold; blue-eyed and odd-eyed versions exist in white cats
- **Lifespan**: 12–20 years

## Health Considerations
- **Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)**: Elevated risk; echocardiogram screening recommended for breeding cats
- **Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD)**: Some bloodlines carry the gene; DNA testing available
- **Obesity**: The combination of a stocky build, dense coat that masks weight, and calm temperament makes weight management especially important
- **Hemophilia B**: Blood clotting disorder more prevalent in British Shorthairs than other breeds; genetic test available
- **Dental Disease**: Prone to gingivitis and periodontal disease; regular dental care essential
- **Arthritis**: Heavier body type increases joint stress with age; maintain healthy weight

## Care Requirements
- **Grooming**: Weekly brushing is usually sufficient; increases to 2–3 times weekly during seasonal shedding (spring and autumn); dense coat benefits from a slicker brush
- **Exercise**: Low to moderate activity; encourage daily interactive play to prevent obesity; not prone to self-exercise
- **Diet**: High-quality protein diet with carefully managed portions; avoid free-feeding; prone to obesity which shortens lifespan
- **Environment**: Adapts well to indoor life; suitable for apartments; does not require outdoor access
- **Social Needs**: Content alone for moderate periods; pairs well with another calm cat if owners work full-time
- **Dental Care**: Regular tooth brushing; veterinary dental cleanings as needed

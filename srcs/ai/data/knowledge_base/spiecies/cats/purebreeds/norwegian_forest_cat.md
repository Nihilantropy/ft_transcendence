# Norwegian Forest Cat

## Overview
The Norwegian Forest Cat (Norsk Skogkatt) is a natural breed from Norway, adapted over centuries to survive the harsh Scandinavian climate. Featured in Norse mythology as the "skogkatt" (forest cat) ridden by the Norse goddess Freya, they are a national symbol of Norway. Their dense, waterproof double coat, tufted ears, and sturdy build reflect their origins as working farm cats. Despite similarities to the Maine Coon, the Norwegian Forest Cat is a genetically distinct breed and is believed to be an ancestor of the Maine Coon through Viking-era trade routes.

## Temperament
- **Calm and Balanced**: Even-tempered, quiet, and adaptable; not easily stressed
- **Friendly but Independent**: Affectionate without being overly clingy; appreciates companionship on their own terms
- **Gentle**: Patient and tolerant; good with children and other pets
- **Intelligent**: Curious and observant; learns environmental routines quickly
- **Moderately Playful**: Enjoys interactive play but less intense than Bengals or Abyssinians; playfulness moderates with age
- **Outdoor Heritage**: Strong predatory instincts; benefits from supervised outdoor time or a secure catio

## Physical Characteristics
- **Size**: Large (females 9–12 lbs; males 13–20 lbs); slow to mature, reaching full size at 5 years
- **Build**: Muscular, substantial body with strong hindquarters — noticeably longer hind legs give a distinctive slanted posture when standing (higher at the rump than the shoulders)
- **Coat**: Dense, waterproof double coat with a coarse, water-shedding outer layer and thick, woolly undercoat; semi-long to long; changes dramatically between summer (shorter) and winter (full coat with mane, bib, and ear tufts)
- **Ruff/Mane**: Full chest ruff in winter coat; less pronounced than Maine Coon ruff
- **Ears**: Medium-large, with inner ear tufts (lynx tips) and outer ear furnishings; positioned at the corner of the head (different from Maine Coon ear placement)
- **Paws**: Large, round, heavily tufted between the toes — natural snowshoes
- **Tail**: Long, bushy, carried high
- **Colors**: All colors and patterns except colorpoint; tabby patterns and bicolors are most common
- **Lifespan**: 12–16 years

## Health Considerations
- **Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)**: Elevated risk; annual cardiac screening recommended for breeding cats
- **Glycogen Storage Disease Type IV (GSD IV)**: Autosomal recessive metabolic disorder causing glycogen accumulation; lethal in kittens or causes progressive neuromuscular disease; DNA test available — carrier testing of breeding cats is essential
- **Hip Dysplasia**: Larger body frame increases susceptibility; monitor for hind limb lameness
- **Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD)**: Reported in some Norwegian Forest Cat lines; DNA test available
- **Obesity**: Dense coat can mask weight gain; regular body condition scoring is important
- **Obesity-Related Diabetes**: Secondary risk from unmanaged weight gain

## Care Requirements
- **Grooming**: Brushing 2–3 times weekly year-round; daily during the spring coat blow (when the entire undercoat is shed); the waterproof outer layer sheds water and debris naturally but the undercoat mats if neglected; pay attention to belly and flanks
- **Exercise**: Moderate to high activity needs; enjoys climbing, hunting games, and outdoor exploration; taller cat trees and outdoor runs are ideal
- **Outdoor Access**: Benefits from supervised outdoor time or a secure catio enclosure; instinctive hunter if allowed to roam
- **Cold Tolerance**: Handles cold weather well due to dense coat; less tolerant of heat — ensure cool, shaded areas in summer
- **Diet**: High-quality protein diet; portion control to prevent obesity; body condition is difficult to assess visually through thick coat — feel ribs regularly
- **Mental Stimulation**: Puzzle feeders, interactive play, and nature-viewing opportunities from windows
- **Genetic Testing**: GSD IV DNA testing is critical before purchasing from a breeder — both parents must be tested

# Siamese

## Overview
The Siamese is one of the oldest and most recognized cat breeds in the world, originating from Thailand (formerly Siam) where they were kept as sacred temple cats. They are famous for their striking color-point coat pattern, piercing blue eyes, and exceptionally vocal nature. The Siamese has been a foundational breed for many modern cat breeds including the Balinese, Tonkinese, Oriental Shorthair, and Himalayan.

## Temperament
- **Highly Vocal**: Loud, insistent voice (the "Meezer" yowl) — they will hold lengthy conversations and demand attention
- **Social**: Cannot tolerate being left alone for long periods; bonds intensely with one or two people
- **Intelligent**: Among the most intelligent cat breeds; requires constant mental stimulation
- **Playful**: Remains active and playful throughout their lives
- **Affectionate**: Extremely people-oriented; often described as "cat-dogs"
- **Opinionated**: Strong personalities; not recommended for owners who prefer a quiet, independent cat

## Physical Characteristics
- **Size**: Medium (6–14 lbs)
- **Body Type**: Long, slender, tubular body (the "Oriental" type); elegant and athletic
- **Coat**: Short, fine, silky coat with minimal shedding
- **Color Points**: Darker coloring (points) on the face/mask, ears, paws, and tail; body is pale
- **Traditional Point Colors**: Seal point (dark brown/black), blue point (grey), chocolate point, lilac point
- **Eyes**: Almond-shaped, vivid blue — the deeper the blue, the more prized
- **Ears**: Large, wide-based, pointed — giving a wedge-shaped appearance
- **Lifespan**: 12–20 years (one of the longest-lived breeds)

## Health Considerations
- **Amyloidosis**: Abnormal protein deposits in organs, particularly the liver; can lead to organ failure; genetic basis suspected
- **Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA)**: Inherited eye disease causing progressive vision loss
- **Mediastinal Lymphoma**: Siamese have elevated risk for this form of lymphoma compared to other breeds
- **Crossed Eyes (Strabismus)**: Historically common; less prevalent in modern breeding lines but still possible
- **Dental Disease**: Prone to periodontal disease; regular dental care essential
- **Respiratory Issues**: Sensitive to cold and respiratory infections; keep indoors in controlled temperatures
- **Separation Anxiety**: Psychological health concern — severe stress when left alone frequently

## Care Requirements
- **Companionship**: Should not be left alone for extended periods; best kept with another cat or in a household with people home frequently
- **Mental Stimulation**: Puzzle toys, interactive play, clicker training — boredom leads to destructive behavior and vocalization
- **Exercise**: High energy; daily interactive play sessions required (wand toys, fetch, laser)
- **Grooming**: Minimal — weekly brushing sufficient due to short coat; ears should be checked regularly
- **Diet**: High-quality protein diet; measure portions as they can be prone to weight gain with age
- **Dental Care**: Regular tooth brushing or dental treats; veterinary cleanings as needed
- **Environment**: Indoor only; sensitive to cold; thrive best in warm, stimulating environments

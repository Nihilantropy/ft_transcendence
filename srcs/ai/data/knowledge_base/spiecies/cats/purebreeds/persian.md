# Persian

## Overview
Persians are one of the oldest and most recognizable cat breeds, originating in Persia (modern-day Iran) and popularized in Europe in the 17th century. Known for their luxurious long coats, flat brachycephalic faces, and calm demeanor, they are the quintessential lap cat and consistently rank among the world's most popular breeds.

## Temperament
- **Calm**: Quiet, gentle, and undemanding — thrives in peaceful, predictable environments
- **Affectionate**: Deeply devoted to their owners but selective in showing affection
- **Adaptable**: Perfectly content indoors; not overly active or curious
- **Reserved**: Shy around strangers; prefers familiar people and routines
- **Non-vocal**: Generally quiet, rarely meow excessively

## Physical Characteristics
- **Size**: Medium to large (7–12 lbs)
- **Coat**: Long, thick, flowing double coat with dense undercoat; requires daily grooming
- **Colors**: Extensive range — white, black, blue, cream, red, chocolate, lilac, silver, golden, and many tabby and bi-color patterns
- **Face**: Flat (brachycephalic) with large round eyes and short snub nose; the doll-face variety has a less extreme profile
- **Eyes**: Large, round, typically copper or blue; white Persians may have blue, copper, or odd-eyes
- **Lifespan**: 12–17 years

## Health Considerations
- **Brachycephalic Obstructive Airway Syndrome (BOAS)**: Flat facial structure causes breathing difficulties, snoring, heat intolerance, and exercise intolerance
- **Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD)**: Autosomal dominant genetic condition causing kidney cysts; DNA testing available — responsible breeders test all breeding cats
- **Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA)**: Inherited eye disease leading to progressive vision loss and eventual blindness
- **Dental Malocclusion**: Misaligned teeth due to compressed skull; regular dental checkups essential
- **Skin Fold Dermatitis**: Infections can develop in facial skin folds; daily cleaning required
- **Epiphora (Eye Discharge)**: Excessive tearing due to blocked tear ducts from facial structure; daily cleaning prevents staining and infection
- **Obesity**: Sedentary temperament combined with calorie-dense diets increases risk

## Care Requirements
- **Grooming**: Daily brushing is non-negotiable to prevent mats and tangles; professional grooming every 4–6 weeks recommended; some owners opt for a "lion cut" in summer
- **Eye Cleaning**: Daily wipe with a soft damp cloth to prevent staining and secondary infection
- **Facial Fold Cleaning**: Clean skin folds around the nose and eye corners regularly to prevent bacterial growth
- **Exercise**: Low activity level; short daily play sessions are sufficient; avoid strenuous activity in heat
- **Environment**: Indoor only — long coat and temperament are not suited to outdoor life; sensitive to temperature extremes
- **Diet**: High-quality protein-rich diet; avoid overfeeding due to sedentary lifestyle; flat-faced kibble shapes may help with eating
